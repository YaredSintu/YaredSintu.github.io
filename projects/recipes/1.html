Introduction

This recipe for Doro Wat holds a special place in our family, often prepared during festive occasions and gatherings. It is typically made with chicken pieces stewed in a flavorful sauce made from onions, garlic, ginger, and a blend of spices such as berbere. Doro wat is often served with injera, a traditional Ethiopian flatbread, and is a staple dish in Ethiopian celebrations and holidays. As my grandmother always said, "Doro Wat brings us together with its rich flavors and heritage."

Total Preperation Time Servings
1 hour 15 minutes 4-6 servings
Ingredients Instructions
  • 2 lbs chicken drumsticks or thighs, skin removed
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 3 tablespoons berbere spice blend
  • 1/4 cup niter kibbeh (spiced clarified butter) or vegetable oil
  • 1 cup chicken broth or water
  • 4 hard-boiled eggs, peeled (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  1. In a large pot, heat the niter kibbeh or oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until they are golden brown.
  3. Add the garlic and ginger, and cook for another minute.
  4. Add the berbere spice blend and stir well to coat the onions.
  5. Add the chicken pieces and brown them on all sides.
  6. Pour in the chicken broth or water, and bring to a boil.
  7. Reduce the heat, cover, and simmer for about 45 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through.
  8. If using, add the hard-boiled eggs during the last 10 minutes of cooking.
  9. Season with salt, pepper, and lemon juice to taste.
  10. Serve hot with injera.

Tips and Tricks

  • For a richer flavor, marinate the chicken in lemon juice and salt for at least an hour before cooking.
  • Adjust the amount of berbere spice according to your heat preference.
  • Using niter kibbeh adds a traditional depth of flavor, but vegetable oil works as a substitute.
  • Serve with traditional Ethiopian injera bread to soak up the delicious sauce.
