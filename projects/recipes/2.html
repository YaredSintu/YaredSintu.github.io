Tibs

Introduction

Tibs is a beloved Ethiopian dish that features sautéed meat, typically beef or lamb, cooked with a medley of vegetables and spices. It is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed on various occasions, from casual family meals to special gatherings. The meat is often seared to perfection and combined with onions, garlic, peppers, and tomatoes, creating a flavorful and aromatic dish. Tibs is commonly served with injera, the traditional Ethiopian flatbread, making it a hearty and satisfying meal. As my father used to say, "Tibs is the taste of home, full of warmth and tradition."

Total Preperation Time Servings
30 minutes 4 servings
Ingredients Instructions
  • 1 lb beef or lamb, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or niter kibbeh (spiced clarified butter)
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon berbere spice blend
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh rosemary or thyme (optional)
  1. Heat the oil or niter kibbeh in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the sliced onions and sauté until they become translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the beef or lamb pieces to the skillet and cook until browned on all sides.
  5. Add the sliced green bell pepper and chopped tomato to the skillet.
  6. Sprinkle the berbere spice blend over the mixture and stir well to coat everything evenly.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. If using, add fresh rosemary or thyme for extra flavor.
  8. Cook for an additional 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and the meat is cooked through.
  9. Remove from heat and let it rest for a few minutes before serving.
  10. Serve hot with injera or rice. Enjoy your Tibs!

Tips and Tricks

  • For a spicier version, increase the amount of berbere spice blend to your taste.
  • You can add other vegetables like mushrooms, zucchini, or carrots for more variety.
  • Marinate the meat with a bit of lemon juice and salt for 30 minutes before cooking to enhance the flavor.
